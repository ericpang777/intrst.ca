CUT BACK ON COSTS

June 28th, 2017

Are you searching for ways to save up for your upcoming spring break trip? Maybe a laptop you have always wanted? Follow these simple steps and you’ll be able to achieve your budgeting goals in no time.

Spending

  • Try to track how much you spend and most importantly WHAT you spend on, that way you can eliminate the “extra” expenses.
  • Do things you can do yourself at home, what does that mean? Make your own sandwich or coffee.
  • Cut cable. Who needs it anyway when you have Netflix?
  • Try to plan out your meals out for the week over the weekend. This way you would not need to waste money on outside food.
  • Most importantly, start budgeting.

Shopping

  • Hunt for sales! Retail stores ALWAYS have a sale going on, whether it’s during the end of the winter or beginning of fall.
  • Buy in bulk, shops like bulk burn and Costco can cure your cravings for less.
  • Look for coupons online, certain stores such as Michael’s craft store and Walmart have monthly coupons.
  • Love shopping for new trends but hate spending too much on something that might go out of style soon? My answer to that is THRIFTING, trust me, there’s no way you will ever twin with anyone with those unique pieces.
  • Whilst shopping for groceries, try to shop when you’re full. When we’re hungry, we tend to buy things we want rather than need.

As teenagers, it is crucial for us to learn to budget at a young age. Having a “spending plan” accounts for planning for your expenses, cutting excess costs, and saving for future long term or short term goals. Budgeting is quite a complex procedure, yet with these easy tips, you will be well on your way towards buying that new laptop or your dream spring break vacation.
