OSAP: WHAT IS IT AND HOW CAN IT HELP YOU?

June 28th, 2017

Are you worried about paying for your post-secondary education? You may have heard of something called OSAP. But what is it really and how can OSAP help you? Read on to learn about the basics of OSAP.

What is OSAP?

OSAP or the Ontario Student Assistance Program is a program funded by the Ontario and federal government that provides grants and loans to Ontario students to help pay for university or college. A grant is money that you donâ€™t have to pay back, while a student loan is money you have to pay back once you graduate. Some students choose only to receive grants. When you apply, the amount and mix of money in grants and loans you receive is determined by several factors such as your personal financial situation, education expenses, and course load. You can check out the OSAP estimator to find how much you could receive.

Am I eligible for OSAP?

As long as you are a student who is a Canadian citizen or a permanent resident of Ontario, you can apply. To determine if you are eligible to receive funds, the government examines the factors listed here. Students who already have the financial resources to cover their tuition may not qualify for OSAP. For example, if your family has a very high household income, or if you have already received another form of government aid that covers your tuition.

How do I apply?

You can easily apply for OSAP online. There are several steps you need to follow and deadlines you need to meet including gathering the right documents.

With OSAP, it may be easier for you to pay for your tuition, and help you on your way to your dream job, Visit the official OSAP website to apply or learn more information.
