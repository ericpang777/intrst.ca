TWO SIDES, ONE COIN

By Simon Lin

Traditional banks have been around for centuries and have proven to be reliable. They operate with hundreds of branch locations in various cities. They provide a location for you to deposit and withdraw money in addition to offering you financial services. Although with the up rise of non-traditional banks, some may ask what the differences between the two institutions are, and which bank best caters to my needs?

Royal Bank of Canada, TD Canada Trust, Canadian Imperial Bank of Canada, Scotiabank and Bank of Montreal are the Big 5 banks of Canada. These banks are the most reliable and have the most ATM locations. With multiple branches in every city, these banks will be at your service. If anything goes wrong, a representative will be right by your side, resolving the issue. The catch? These banks will provide accounts with lower interest rates than non-traditional banks, which means less money in the long-run.

Tangerine Financial and PC (President's Choice) Financial are two of the largest non-traditional banks in Canada. These banks have limited brick and mortar branches in comparison to traditional banks. Instead, they mainly operate online but do have access to a national network of ATM machines (Scotiabank and CIBC, respectively) where you have the full menu of services.  This allows these emerging banks to offer you higher interest rates and many more no-fee transactions (like chequing, withdraws, etc.) than traditional student banks accounts.  For the privilege of not paying for expensive physical branch networks, you get access to more features for less money and still have great customer service via web chat, 1800 phone numbers and solid mobile banking apps.  We are a generation that lives on our mobile handsets, our laptops an sometime even the phone.  You should absolutely check out Tangerine Financial and PC Financial.

Depending on the use of your account, you can choose either type of bank. I personally bank with a Big 5 bank with both a chequing account and a savings account there. It provides convenience when I access cash and when I monitor my accounts. If you need a bank to simply deposit money, I recommend banking a non-traditional bank. If you need a bank that you can conveniently access, I recommend banking with a Big 5 bank. On the other hand, if you don’t mind having your cash in two different banks, I recommend banking with both types. You can get high interest rates while easily accessing your cash. Win-win?
