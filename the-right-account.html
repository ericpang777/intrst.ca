THE RIGHT ACCOUNT FOR YOU

By Sharon Zheng

THE RIGHT ACCOUNT FOR YOU

June 28th, 2017

Have you ever wondered what type of bank account is most suitable for you? Are you the type of person who tends to overspend money, or do you rarely make monetary transactions? Here are some aspects of chequing and savings accounts, to help you decide on what account to choose.

Chequing Account

A chequing account is an account for everyday transactions. You could call a chequing account, a “base-building account”. It allows you to make often unlimited deposits and numerous withdrawals quickly and easily. This means chequing accounts are very liquid because they can immediately provide cash. Chequing accounts are convenient and accessible. You can easily access your money through cheques, ATM’s, debit cards, and through online banking. Do you have a job? Direct deposit is a method employers use to electronically deposit your paycheck into your bank account. Do you have an allowance? Electronic funds transfer (EFTs), provide an easy way for parents to transfer money from their account to yours.

Savings Account

Savings accounts are used primarily for saving up for major expenses, such as university tuition, or storing excess funds. This is the type of account you would use to store money safely in a financial institution, or simply if you don’t make payments on a regular daily basis. This account can also be beneficial when your chequing account is denied or for financial planning purposes in the long-term. A regular savings account is simply cash equivalent, and withdrawals can occur at any time and frequency. There is liquidity and no limited terms. It’s a great way to earn interest, as well. Since a basic savings accounts only provides little interest, though, you could consider a Guaranteed Investment Certificate (GIC). In a GIC, you would earn higher interest rates, but the minimum value to have in your account is higher.
